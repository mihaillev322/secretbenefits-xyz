Platform Features and Account Management

Secret Benefits operates through a credit-based economic model where male members purchase credits to access premium communication features while female members enjoy free access to most platform capabilities. This asymmetric pricing reflects the supply-demand dynamics inherent in sugar dating, where female members are typically in higher demand and shorter supply.

The platform's core features include advanced search filters allowing users to specify age ranges, locations, body types, relationship expectations, and lifestyle preferences. The messaging system supports text communication with photo sharing capabilities, though the platform notably lacks video chat features found on some competitors. Profile customization options include multiple photo uploads, detailed written descriptions, and tags indicating interests and arrangement preferences.

Account verification involves submitting a photo while holding a sign with your username and current date. This manual review process typically completes within 24 hours and adds a 'verified' badge to profiles, significantly increasing response rates. According to platform data, verified profiles receive 3.4 times more messages than unverified ones. However, verification only confirms photo authenticity, not identity, income, or background.

For users seeking to delete their accounts, the process requires accessing account settings and selecting permanent deletion rather than temporary deactivation. This distinction matters because deletion removes all data and forfeits unused credits, while deactivation simply hides your profile. The complete process is outlined on our FAQ page, which provides step-by-step guidance for various account management tasks. Users concerned about digital privacy should note that deleted data may remain in backup systems for up to 90 days per the platform's data retention policy.

Safety Guidelines and Best Practices

Personal safety should remain the primary concern for anyone participating in sugar dating. While Secret Benefits implements platform-level security measures, individual users bear responsibility for their own safety decisions. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reports that approximately 1 in 6 women and 1 in 19 men have experienced stalking victimization during their lifetime, with online connections representing an increasing percentage of these cases.

Before meeting anyone from the platform, conduct thorough vetting through extended messaging over several weeks. Request video calls through external platforms like FaceTime or Skype to confirm identity, though respect if someone needs time to build trust first. Search their photos through reverse image search tools to identify potential catfishing. Never send money, gift cards, or financial information to someone you haven't met in person, regardless of their story or emergency.

First meetings should always occur in public locations during daytime hours. Coffee shops, restaurants in busy areas, or public parks provide safe neutral ground. Inform a trusted friend of your plans, including the person's profile information, meeting location, and expected return time. Consider sharing your live location through your phone. Arrange your own transportation rather than accepting rides, and keep your phone charged and accessible.

Financial arrangements require clear, explicit discussion before any physical intimacy occurs. Successful sugar relationships involve ongoing communication about expectations, boundaries, and any changes to the arrangement terms. According to research from the University of California, arrangements with clearly defined terms and regular check-in conversations last an average of 8.3 months compared to 3.1 months for those without explicit agreements. Our home page provides additional context about arrangement structures and communication strategies.

Be aware of common scam patterns: requests for money before meeting, stories about emergencies requiring immediate financial help, pressure to move conversations off-platform quickly, reluctance to video chat or meet in person, and requests for gift cards or cryptocurrency. The FBI's Internet Crime Complaint Center received over 19,000 romance scam reports in 2023 resulting in $739 million in losses, making this the highest-loss category of online fraud.

For additional safety resources, consult Department of Justice guidance on identifying and avoiding romance scams.
